## $650 Billion Bet: AI Is Real Infrastructure Now — but 70–85% of Projects Still Fail

*Geopolitical Intelligence Brief · April 8, 2026*

**AI Boom — Usage, Reality Check, Enterprise, Data Centers & Global Power**

The four largest US hyperscalers (Alphabet, Amazon, Meta, Microsoft) are on track to spend $635–665 billion on AI infrastructure in 2026 alone — a 67–74% spike from 2025 and a figure that exceeds the GDP of most nations (Bloomberg). Global data center capacity is set to double by 2030, requiring $3 trillion in investment. And yet: 70–85% of enterprise AI projects still fail to meet expected outcomes, 42% of companies abandoned most AI initiatives in 2025 (up from 17% in 2024), and MIT Sloan researchers are publicly calling the AI industry's similarities to the dot-com bubble "hard to ignore." The paradox of 2026 is not whether AI is real — it is — but whether the infrastructure being built will generate returns before the capital markets run out of patience. Meanwhile, China's DeepSeek has democratised AI for the Global South, India is positioning as a "third way," and the data center power problem may be the binding constraint that no amount of capital can solve quickly enough.

> **📐 Lens: Carlota Perez Technology Surge + Gartner Trough of Disillusionment** — Every general-purpose technology follows: installation boom → financial bubble → crash → deployment phase where real productivity materialises. AI is in the installation boom, with the bubble and deployment phase both ahead.

**Confirmed Winners (now):** `Nvidia (90% GPU share)` `Cloud Hyperscalers` `Coding / Dev Tools` `China (DeepSeek)`

**Demand Failing (now):** `Agentic AI (overhyped)` `Enterprise GenAI ROI` `Power Grid Growth` `Global South Access`

## ✅ Real vs 🚫 Fake — AI Use Case Audit 2026

### ✅ Real Use Cases (Measurable ROI)

*   **Software development / coding:** Developers code up to 55% faster with GitHub Copilot. 90% of software professionals now use AI tools. Pull requests +8.69% per developer. *(Fullview\.io / Harvard Business School)*

*   **Financial services data analysis:** State Street reports 20–30% better prediction outcomes. Bloomberg cites 60% better measurement of macro criteria using AI on unstructured data. *(Bloomberg Enterprise Data Summit, Nov 2025)*

*   **Customer service / contact centres:** Telstra reduced customer follow-ups by 20%. A company with 5,000 agents saw 14% higher issue resolution per hour. *(AI Assistant Statistics 2026 / index.dev)*

*   **Medical diagnosis (narrow):** Microsoft's MAI-DxO achieved 85.5% accuracy in medical cases vs 20% for experienced doctors in specific diagnostic tasks. *(AI Assistant Statistics 2026)*

*   **Recruiting / HR screening:** 66% use AI for job descriptions; 89% of HR pros say it saves time. Leading measurable enterprise use case. *(SHRM / Talent Trends 2025)*

*   **Manufacturing / industrial AI:** Robotics, autonomous vehicles, drone operations reshaping logistics and defence. Adoption especially advanced. *(Deloitte State of AI Enterprise 2026)*

*   **Marketing copy / content generation:** 40% productivity boost reported. Harvard study: tasks completed 25.1% faster with 40%+ higher quality. *(Harvard Business School / McKinsey 2025)*

### 🚫 Failing / Overhyped Use Cases

*   **Agentic AI for complex business:** Anthropic and Carnegie Mellon experiments find agents "make too many mistakes for businesses to rely on for any process involving big money." MIT Sloan: GenAI is now in the Gartner Trough of Disillusionment. *(MIT Sloan Management Review 2026)*

*   **Enterprise-wide transformation:** Only 6% of organisations qualify as "AI high performers" (5%+ EBIT impact). 42% abandoned most AI initiatives in 2025. 70–85% of projects fail to meet expected outcomes. *(Fullview\.io / McKinsey / Deloitte 2026)*

*   **Revenue generation:** 74% of organisations still hope AI will grow revenue — only 20% are actually doing so. AI has delivered efficiency gains, not yet growth. *(Deloitte State of AI Enterprise 2026)*

*   **Microsoft Copilot for office productivity:** MIT Sloan: tools like Copilot have resulted in "incremental and mostly unmeasurable productivity gains." The ROI is not showing up in company financials. *(MIT Sloan Management Review 2026)*

*   **AI replacing knowledge workers at scale:** AI triples productivity on only \~⅓ of tasks. "Job hugging" up: 64% of workers hide tasks from AI to protect their roles. *(ManpowerGroup Global Talent Barometer 2026)*

*   **ROI measurement itself:** 23% of organisations have no mechanism to measure AI ROI at all. 66% struggle to establish ROI metrics. 80% of firms see no bottom-line impact. *(SHRM / Autofaceless AI Statistics 2026)*

*   **AI hallucinations solved:** 77% of businesses still express concern about hallucinations. 47% of enterprise users made at least one major decision based on hallucinated content in 2024. *(Fullview\.io AI Statistics 2026)*

## 🌐 Party Perspectives

### 🇺🇸 United States

*   **Economics & Demand:** AI CapEx drove over one-third of US GDP growth in first 9 months of 2025. But three of four hyperscalers lost market value after recent earnings calls despite massive spending commitments. Markets fear gap between investment and revenue. *(Bloomberg)*
*   **Infrastructure & Data Centers:** US drives \~90% of Americas data center capacity (\~50% of global supply). New data centers under construction will increase US peak power demand by nearly 20% after two decades of flat growth. *(Bismarck / IEA / JLL)*
*   **Real Use Cases vs Hype:** Real wins: coding (55% speed gain), financial services, narrow medical AI. Failing: Copilot ROI unmeasurable (MIT Sloan), agentic AI not ready. CFO revolt has begun — 42% abandoned most AI initiatives in 2025. *(MIT Sloan / Bloomberg)*
*   **Sovereignty & Global Power:** US AI export controls "inherently porous" (CSIS). DeepSeek proved China achieved near-GPT-4 without US chips, potentially invalidating export control strategy. *(CSIS / MIT Sloan)*

### 🇨🇳 China

*   **Economics & Demand:** DeepSeek V3.2, Kimi K2.5, and MiniMax M2.5 occupy three of top five global AI models by usage. China's R\&D intensity in AI is globally leading. DeepSeek open-sourced R1 model at fraction of US costs. *(Xinhua BFA 2026)*
*   **Infrastructure & Data Centers:** APAC data center capacity grows from 32 GW to 57 GW by 2030 (12% CAGR). China builds its own full AI stack: chips (Huawei/SMIC), models (DeepSeek/Qwen/Kimi), cloud (Alibaba/Tencent). *(CSIS)*
*   **Real Use Cases vs Hype:** China leads in industrial automation, smart cities, surveillance — applications where West faces ethical constraints. Unitree (robotics) profitable and scaling. Baidu's Apollo further along than US equivalents. *(ORF / CSIS)*
*   **Sovereignty & Global Power:** DeepSeek is China's most effective geopolitical AI tool — open-sourcing builds global dependency, positions China as "democratic" alternative, embeds Chinese values at 88% censorship rate. *(ORF / China-Global South Project)*

### 🇷🇺 Russia

*   **Economics & Demand:** Russia's AI economy severely constrained by sanctions cutting off US chips and cloud access. Domestic AI investment (Yandex, Sber AI) operates in isolation. Russia is consumer of AI, not producer at global scale. *(MIT Sloan / Atlantic Council)*
*   **Infrastructure & Data Centers:** Russian data center development limited by sanctions and chip access. Increasingly relies on Chinese hardware (Huawei servers), creating hardware dependency on China. *(CSIS / Atlantic Council)*
*   **Real Use Cases vs Hype:** Russia's real AI use case is military: drone guidance, EW coordination, battlefield intelligence. RT frames AI as "a weapon, not a productivity tool" — serves Russian strategic interests. *(PIIE / Atlantic Council)*
*   **Sovereignty & Global Power:** Russia promotes AI governance through BRICS excluding Western oversight. BRICS AI Charter counters OECD/EU AI Act. Russia frames US export controls as "technological imperialism." *(BRICS / MIT Sloan)*

### 🇮🇳 India

*   **Economics & Demand:** India's AI economy growing but punching below weight. IndiaAI Mission allocated ₹10,300 crore (\~$1.2B) — fraction of China/US spend. India will not have a "DeepSeek moment" anytime soon. *(Carnegie Endowment / AFP)*
*   **Infrastructure & Data Centers:** Microsoft, Google, Amazon announced billion-dollar India data center investments at Modi's AI Summit (Feb 2026). India remains dependent on US cloud infrastructure. Trajectory: "world's largest AI adoption market" — consumer, not producer. *(Rest of World / AFP)*
*   **Real Use Cases vs Hype:** India's real successes are digital public infrastructure: Aadhaar, UPI, Bhashini (multilingual AI). Sarvam AI released LLMs trained from scratch — serving India's linguistic diversity, not competing with GPT. *(Rest of World / Carnegie)*
*   **Sovereignty & Global Power:** India positioned as "third way" AI model — neither US corporate capture nor Chinese state surveillance. Attempting to become template for Global South AI adoption. *(Rest of World / Economic Times)*

### 🇩🇪 Europe

*   **Economics & Demand:** Europe is "AI laggard" in infrastructure investment. EMEA data center CAGR of 10% lags US (17%). German industrial AI disrupted by cheaper Chinese alternatives. *(JLL / ECB)*
*   **Infrastructure & Data Centers:** EMEA will add 13 GW new supply by 2030. EU AI Act makes data center location decisions complex — data sovereignty means EU cannot freely use US cloud AI without compliance risk. *(JLL / EU AI Act)*
*   **Real Use Cases vs Hype:** Europe's strongest AI use case is regulatory — EU AI Act is world's most comprehensive framework becoming global baseline ("Brussels Effect"). European labs (Mistral, Aleph Alpha) are genuine frontier models. *(Der Spiegel / EU AI Act)*
*   **Sovereignty & Global Power:** EU AI Act creates "Brussels Effect" — third countries seeking EU market access must comply with EU standards, exporting European AI governance globally. *(EU AI Act / Bloomberg)*

### 🇯🇵 Japan

*   **Economics & Demand:** Japan's AI investment accelerating after years of underperformance. Japan-India AI co-development agreement signed. GPIF ($1.5T+) redirecting to include AI infrastructure as strategic asset class. *(NHK World / Bloomberg)*
*   **Infrastructure & Data Centers:** Japan's grid fragmented between 50Hz and 60Hz regions, limiting power deployment. Nuclear reactor restarts fast-tracked during Hormuz crisis now available for data centers. *(METI / NHK)*
*   **Real Use Cases vs Hype:** Japan's real AI strengths: robotics (Fanuc, Yaskawa), precision manufacturing, semiconductor equipment. Cultural approach — "human-centred automation" — produces more conservative but reliable deployments. *(NHK World)*
*   **Sovereignty & Global Power:** Japan signed AI co-development agreement with India — building non-US, non-China AI axis. Quad partnership commits to US export control alignment, but Japan hedges with bilateral AI relationships. *(NHK / Nikkei Asia)*

### 🌍 Global South

*   **Economics & Demand:** "Without deliberate intervention, concentration of AI infrastructure in advanced economies will widen existing inequalities. Global South risks becoming consumer rather than co-creator." — Sam Daws, Oxford Martin AI Initiative. *(Xinhua BFA 2026)*
*   **Infrastructure & Data Centers:** Sub-Saharan Africa and Southeast Asia data centers years behind demand. US cloud providers anchor regional hubs — creating dependency, not sovereignty. China's approach (DeepSeek + Huawei) embeds Chinese infrastructure. *(CSIS / Xinhua)*
*   **Real Use Cases vs Hype:** Real Global South AI: agricultural yield prediction (India's Kisan AI), climate adaptation, mobile health diagnostics, local language NLP. Deployment of existing AI, not frontier development. *(ORF / Rest of World)*
*   **Sovereignty & Global Power:** Global South is geopolitical prize in AI sovereignty wars. US wants export controls extended; China promotes open-source AI as "democratic"; India promotes "third way" digital public infrastructure. *(CSIS / ORF)*

## 🏗️ Data Center Deep Dive — Can They Really Grow?

### 📈 Supply: Growing Fast

100 GW of new capacity 2026–2030, doubling global supply. CAGR of 14% globally through 2030. Americas leads at 17% CAGR. Data center capex crossed $1 trillion in 2026 — expected by 2029 just a year ago. Over 23 GW under construction globally at end of Q3 2025. *(JLL / Dell'Oro / BloombergNEF)*

### ⚡ Constraint: Power is the Binding Limit

Power — not capital — is the #1 bottleneck. US data centers will drive nearly half of all US power demand growth through 2030. Data center construction costs: $11.30M per MW in 2026, rising 6% vs 2025. 60% of professionals expect 5–15% cost rises in 2026. *(IEA / UN / Avid Solutions)*

### 🤔 Demand: Real but Front-Loaded by Training

AI only represented \~25% of all data center workloads in 2025 — training dominated. Real shift: inference overtakes training in 2027 as models are deployed. By 2030, AI could be 50% of all workloads. Half of planned US data center builds already delayed or cancelled due to energy and parts shortages. *(JLL / Tom's Hardware / Network World)*

### ⚠️ The Stranded Asset Risk — Can They Really Grow?

MIT Sloan: "It's hard not to see similarities to the dot-com bubble — sky-high valuations, emphasis on user growth over profits, expensive infrastructure buildout." BloombergNEF: neocloud contracts (5-year terms) expose operators to risk if long-term AI demand is lower than expected. Dell'Oro's Baron Fung: "CEOs hesitate to invest fully because they cannot clearly see future AI adoption patterns. Companies face a tough choice: risk stranded assets by overinvesting or fall behind by underinvesting." *(MIT Sloan / BloombergNEF / Dell'Oro Group 2026)*

## 🔭 Forward Forecast — Scenarios & Timelines

### 🟢 Best Case — "Deployment Phase Begins"

*AI earns its spend; inference demand validates infrastructure*

Q1 2026 Mag-7 earnings beat on AI revenue. Inference demand grows as consumer and enterprise AI apps reach mainstream. Power constraints solved by nuclear + gas bridging. By 2027, AI represents 30%+ of enterprise software revenue. The Carlota Perez "deployment phase" begins. *Probability: 30%* *(JLL / Lord Abbett / Deloitte)*

### 🟡 Base Case — "Trough of Disillusionment"

*AI real but ROI patchy; bubble deflates slowly; winners and losers diverge*

AI proves real value in specific use cases (coding, finance, manufacturing) but fails to justify enterprise-wide spending. Data center growth slows from 14% CAGR. DeepSeek and open-source compress AI lab margins. Agentic AI enters Gartner trough. 6% of companies capture 80% of AI value. *Probability: 50%* *(MIT Sloan / Gartner / McKinsey)*

### 🔴 Worst Case — "Dot-com 2.0"

*AI earnings disappoint; hyperscaler CapEx cuts; stranded infrastructure*

One major hyperscaler misses badly on AI revenue. CapEx guidance cut. Nvidia valuation collapses from 90% market-share premium. Neocloud companies — on 5-year leases with no secondary market — face financing crises. Data center construction halts. AI winter 2.0. *Probability: 20%* *(MIT Sloan / BloombergNEF / Network World)*
